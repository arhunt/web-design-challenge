Latitude vs. Temperature

Northern Hemisphere



The high temperatures taken in the Northern Hemisphere summer have a strong inverse correlation to the latitude of the location, with an r-squared value of approximately 0.56.

For example, one can predict that the high temperature at 20 degrees north latitude is around 80 degrees Fahrenheit, and the high temperature at 60 degrees north latitude is around 60 degrees Fahrenheit.
